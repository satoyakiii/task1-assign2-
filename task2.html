About Hedy Lamarr

Hedy Lamarr (1914-2000) was an Austrian-American film actress and inventor. During World War II, together with composer George Antheil, she co-created a frequency-hopping concept aimed at securing radio-guided torpedoes from jamming. The idea later influenced spread-spectrum and wireless technologies. Beyond cinema, Lamarr’s legacy lives on in modern communications.

“Glamour on screen, genius off screen.”

Major Life Events

  1. 1933-1937: Early European film career, rising to international attention.
  2. 1937: Emigrated to the United States; signed with MGM in Hollywood.
  3. 1942: U.S. Patent No. 2,292,387 (with George Antheil) for frequency-hopping system.
  4. 1940s: Acted in numerous films while supporting wartime efforts.
  5. 1997: EFF Pioneer Award.
  6. 2014: Inducted into the National Inventors Hall of Fame.

Key Contributions

  • Frequency-hopping spread spectrum concept to resist jamming.
  • Early foundation for modern Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and GPS techniques.
  • Advocacy for wartime innovation and technology in communications.
